Intro

Welcome, traveler! Thank you for stopping by. My name is Brandon Dela Cruz. I am an inherentely curious and optimistic person fascinated by the world we live in. I graduated from the University of Hawaii at Manoa (UHM) with a Bachelors of Science in Global Environmental Science (GES) and a certificate in Geospatial Information Sciences (GIS).

During my academic journey, I collaborated with GIS professionals on diverse projects. I worked with the City and County of Honolulu Storm Water Quality Division, employing ESRI products for visualizing nutrient concentrations in Oahu's Eastern watershed and creating a StoryMap for public outreach. Additionally, I contributed to a NASA-funded Environmental Justice project at UHM, involving data management and mining. At AECOM, I provided technical support to Engineers and Planners using ArcGIS products, generating field maps from converted CAD files and State Office of Planning data. Lastly, for my GES thesis, I applied my tecnhical skills in Python and QGIS to analyze E. coli spatiotemporal variability in an urban wetland. These experiences have equipped me with a strong foundation in GIS and Data Analysis, ready to contribute to meaningful projects in the field.

Currently I am employed with Element Environmental as an Environmental Scientist. Part of my role includes working creating field maps and survey reports from goverment, federal, and field efforts data to provide various reports in an array of formats including AutoCAD Civil 3D.

Software skills: ESRI suite (Desktop and Pro), QGIS, AutoCAD, Python, Agisoft Metashape
